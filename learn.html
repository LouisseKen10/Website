CAMERA BASICS

Camera basics are the foundation of photography. They focus on the exposure triangle: aperture, shutter speed, and ISO. Aperture controls how much light enters the lens and affects background blur, shutter speed controls motion by freezing or blurring action, and ISO adjusts the camera’s sensitivity to light. Understanding how these three work together allows photographers to capture clear, balanced, and creative images.

COMPOSITION

Composition is the way elements are arranged in a photo to create balance, focus, and meaning. Using techniques like the rule of thirds, leading lines, symmetry, and framing helps guide the viewer’s eye to the subject. Good composition turns a simple shot into a powerful image that tells a story.

ANGLES

Angles change how a subject is seen and felt in a photograph. Shooting from high, low, or unusual viewpoints can add depth, drama, or emphasis to the image. Exploring different angles helps create unique perspectives that make photos more engaging and memorable.

LIGHTING

Lighting is one of the most important elements in photography because it shapes mood, depth, and clarity. Natural light creates soft and realistic effects, while artificial light offers control and creativity. Learning how to use and balance light helps bring out details and highlight the subject effectively.

COLOR

Color adds mood, emotion, and meaning to a photograph. Warm colors like red and yellow create energy and excitement, while cool colors like blue and green bring calmness and peace. Understanding how to use and combine colors helps photographers create stronger visual impact and tell deeper stories.

FOCUS

Focus directs the viewer’s attention to the most important part of a photograph. A sharp subject stands out clearly, while a blurred background or foreground adds depth and emphasis. Mastering focus ensures that images are clear, intentional, and powerful in storytelling.
